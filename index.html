Permanent Dentition

Everything you need to know about permanent teeth.

What is Permanent Dentition?

Permanent dentition refers to the second set of teeth that replace primary (baby) teeth. There are 32 permanent teeth in total, composed of:

  • 8 Incisors (cutting food)
  • 4 Canines (tearing food)
  • 8 Premolars (crushing/tearing)
  • 12 Molars including 4 wisdom teeth (grinding food)

Proper Care

  • Brush 2 minutes using circular motions
  • Floss properly daily
  • Sealants for children's molars
  • Orthodontic evaluation around age 7
  • Healthy eating habits
  • Wear mouthguards during sports

What Happens If Oral Care Is Ignored?

Short-term

  • Tooth decay
  • Gingivitis
  • Bad breath
  • Tooth sensitivity

Long-term

  • Periodontitis (gum recession, bone loss)
  • Tooth loss
  • Dental abscesses
  • Difficulty chewing and speaking
  • Malocclusion
  • Systemic effects (heart disease, diabetes complications)
